# Voice & Tone

## Who's talking
Pole Position speaks like **race control crossed with a cocky rival.** Confident,
fast, a little trash-talky — because the AI model *is* the opponent. The app is
daring you: *think you can beat the model? Prove it.*

This is **not** the @zach_schaefer voice ("fan learning in public"). This is the
product's voice: the model that already locked its picks and is waiting for you.

## Principles
1. **Skill, never gambling.** This is a prediction *game*. You earn points and bragging rights. Never "bet," "odds," "wager," or "stake."
2. **Second person, present tense.** "Lock your picks." "You called it." "The model disagrees."
3. **Short. Punchy. Lights-out.** Cut every word that isn't load-bearing. If a line can't be read in one breath, split it.
4. **The model is the antagonist.** Personify it. It's confident, it's data-driven, and it wants you to lose. Beating it is the whole point.
5. **Earned hype.** Celebrate real wins ("Called It"), not empty hype. No "game-changer," no "revolutionary."

## Lexicon — use these
Lights out · P1 / pole · the grid / the grid is set · podium · fastest lap ·
undercut · chequered flag · DNF · lock in your picks · race control · Called It ·
Beat the model · the model's pick · climb the grid · checkered to checkered.

## Lexicon — avoid these
- **bet / odds / wager / gamble / stake** → it's prediction & skill.
- **the word "clarity"** → permanently banned across all brand copy.
- **generic hype**: game-changer, revolutionary, next-level, unleash.
- **driver names, team names, driver likeness, or F1/team IP** in branded assets, merch, or marketing creative. Pole Position is its own world. (See `logo.md` and merch rules.)
- pundit / expert framing for the *user* — they're a competitor, not an analyst.

## Signature lines
- **"Beat Pole Position — Predict to Win."** (primary tagline)
- **"Called It."** (the win stamp)
- **"The model locked its picks. Did you?"**
- **"Lights out. Lock in."**

## Tone by moment
| Moment | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Inviting challenge | "The model thinks it knows the grid. Show it up." |
| Picks open | Urgency, light | "Picks are open. Quali closes Saturday — lock in before the grid is set." |
| Pick locked | Confident confirmation | "Locked. The model's waiting." |
| Correct pick | Earned celebration | "Called It. +5. You read the grid better than the model." |
| Wrong pick | Sting, never shame | "Not this time. The model called P1. Next race — get it back." |
| Leaderboard | Competitive | "You're P3 on the grid. Two races to the front row." |

## Microcopy rules
- Buttons are verbs: **Lock In · Make Your Picks · See the Grid · Climb**.
- Empty states keep the world: "No picks yet. The grid is waiting."
- Errors stay in character but stay helpful: "Couldn't lock that in — pit lane's busy. Try again."
